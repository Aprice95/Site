Marching Tycoon is the game version of everything I love about the marching arts: the grind of rehearsal, the slow build of a program, and the payoff when the season finally comes together. It launched on the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/marching-tycoon/id6777115732) on June 7, and as of this week it's on [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.aaronprice.marchingtycoonand) too — so the whole band is here. It's free on both. 🥁

![The Marching Tycoon field screen, one marcher deep](/images/apps/marching-tycoon-field-start.png)

## How it plays

You start with a single marcher on an empty field. Tapping the metronome earns **Reps** — the game's currency — and tapping *in time* builds a streak that boosts your active play. It's an idle game at heart, though, so the program keeps rehearsing while you're away: close the app, come back later, and bank the progress.

Reps go into the things every band needs:

- **Staff** — start with the practical hires; the later roles get strange in the best way
- **Equipment** — gear changes your tap value, passive production, and how fast the block fills out
- **Seasons** — hit the marcher goal, go to competition, earn a permanent medal, and roll into next season stronger

There are **fifteen competition tiers**, from local shows all the way to galactic finals. The field fills in as you grow, and watching a lone marcher become a packed formation never stops being satisfying.

## Free, with one optional upgrade

The whole game is free to play. There's a single optional in-app purchase — the **Director's Pass** — if you want to support development and speed things up a bit.

Updates have been landing steadily since launch, and a **browser version is in the works** so you'll eventually be able to play on the web too — more on that when it's ready.
